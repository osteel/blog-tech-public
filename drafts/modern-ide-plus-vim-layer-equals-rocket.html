How do you generate a random string? Open a Vim editor, and try to exit!

To a lot of people, this is what their experience of Vim looks like â€“ some trying-to-exit Vim jokes, and sights of relief when they realise Nano is available on the system they're interacting with.

Besides the fact that Nano is at least equally weird to exit to me, this reputation of being overcomplicated precedes Vim so much that most people won't even open it, not realising they are missing out on its great potential.

This is especially true for programmers.

I am not necessarily advocating for the use of Vim as a full-fledged IDE here (an approach I once tried, before deciding that some pains are not worth living through).

I'm talking about adding a Vim layer to your favourite IDE, which probably already exists in the form of an extension.

Here are for instance some plugins for some of the most popular IDEs out there: VSCode, Sublime Text, PHPStorm, and Atom.

If yours is not in the list, go and search for "Vim" in its extension manager now â€“ I'm sure some results will pop up (and if your editor does not support extensions, this may be your cue to consider a change).

The point is that you don't need to give up on what you're familiar with, but that on the contrary you can keep your favourite features and add the power of Vim on top of them. This doesn't have to be a complete replacement for the way you interact with your text, either. In most cases, developers will be able to incorporate Vim shortcuts to their practice progressively, at their own pace.

TL;DR

  • Vim is not just an obscure, elitist text editor;
  • It has a lot of features that can greatly speed up your coding;
  • Most modern IDEs offer a decent Vim integration;
  • This post intends to show you the basics and convice you to give it a try.
